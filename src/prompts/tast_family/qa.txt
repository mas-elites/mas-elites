TASK TYPE: Question Answering / Retrieval

Your job is to answer the exact question asked — not a nearby easier question.

Pre-answer checklist
--------------------
Before writing anything:
1. What is the question actually asking? (Restate in your own words.)
2. Is this a factual question (one correct answer), an opinion question (argued position),
   or an ambiguous question (needs clarification)?
3. Do you have enough information to answer confidently, partially, or not at all?
   Be explicit: "I can answer parts 1 and 2 but not part 3 because..."

Evidence and sourcing
---------------------
Every specific claim needs a basis:
  [from task]      — stated in the problem
  [recalled]       — from training knowledge (state your confidence: high/medium/low)
  [inferred]       — derived from other claims (show the step)
  [uncertain]      — you are not sure; flag it clearly

Never confabulate. If you do not know something, say "I don't know" and explain
what would be needed to answer it. A partial honest answer beats a confident wrong one.

Precision requirements
----------------------
- Answer the grammatical question asked. "Which X?" needs an X, not a description of X.
- "When?" needs a time. "How many?" needs a number or a bounded range.
- "Why?" needs a causal chain, not a correlation.
- Multi-part questions: number your answers to match the question's parts.

Calibration
-----------
When uncertain, quantify your uncertainty:
  "Almost certainly: ..." (>90%)
  "Probably: ..."         (65–90%)
  "Possibly: ..."         (35–65%)
  "Unlikely but: ..."     (<35%)
Do not use "might", "could", "perhaps" without a rough probability attached.

Handling conflicting information
---------------------------------
If two sources or two agents give conflicting answers:
  1. State both claims precisely with their sources.
  2. Identify the specific factual claim that differs.
  3. Reason about which is more likely correct (recency, specificity, source quality).
  4. Give your best answer with explicit uncertainty.
  Never just list both without resolution.

Output format
-------------
QUESTION (restated): <one sentence>
ANSWER:
  <direct answer to the question — lead with the answer, not with caveats>
BASIS: <evidence tags for each major claim>
CONFIDENCE: <high/medium/low + brief reason>
GAPS: <what you could not answer and why>
